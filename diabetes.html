DIABETES

What is diabetes?

Diabetes is currently one of the leading chronic diseases not only in developed countries but also in developing countries. With a prevalence of 78% in the world.

Diabetes in divided into type 1 and 11, with two being the commonest.

It’s not only causing several mortality rates but also paving way for other diseases like hypertension and other related complications. Nevertheless, it exposes one to other conditions due to compromised immunity.

One may wonder what we are even talking about

Both type 1 and type two are metabolic conditions that increase blood sugar and inhibit the production of insulin which is the hormone that regulates the amount of sugar in the body. Both types have almost the same symptoms although may be encountered at different stages of life and have some few differences.

Type 1 Diabetes

Type1 diabetes is an autoimmune disease, in such a case, the body produces antibodies that fight against its own cells, here the antibodies will fight and destroy the cells in the pancreas that produce insulin. This is mainly believed to be caused by genetics and environmental factors. this will mainly surface at childhood or later stages and it’s a life time disease

Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes surfaces when the body becomes resistant to insulin. In other words, the insulin used to respond to increased sugar levels and does the necessary but after some time due to some factors, such a genetics and lifestyle such as obesity and inactivity This usually arises during adulthood and often times, it can be reversed or controlled by exercise and diet. Hope this gives you hope as it does to me.

What are some of the managerial means?

For Type 1 Diabetes

Insulin injections often four times, per day
Blood glucose monitoring
A healthy cholesterol diets
Plenty of exercise, and as per now, there is no way to prevent or cure type 1 diabetes.

For Type 2 Diabetes

Modifying of diet and life style
Having a low cholesterol diet
Regular exercise
In some cases, oral medications may be prescribed to stimulate insulin production
Some patients may also need insulin

What do we all realize by this, the greatest prevalence of the two is type 2 diabetes, and its preventable and managerial means is more of behavior and life style. Together, we can fight this, INHC is here to guide and help you on how we can get through with this.
