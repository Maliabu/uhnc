Hypertension

Non communicable diseases kill 41 million people each year which is equivalent to 71% of all deaths globally.

Each year, more than 15 million people of these are of ages 30-69, 85% of these deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries of which African countries is one of them. Cardiovascular diseases account for most of these death with over 17.9million people dying annually. Hypertension is the major cause of premature death worldwide.

What is Hypetension?

First of all, blood pressure is the force exerted by the circulating blood against the walls of the blood vessels in the body. When this pressure, rises, we get what we call hypertension. ( high blood pressure. Blood pressure is written in two numbers, the first one(systolic), represents the pressure of the blood vessels when the heart contracts. The second (diastolic) number represents the pressure of the blood vessels when the heart relaxes.

What are the normal readings of hypertension?

Prehypertension

Conclusion

If we are all to realize, most of these factors are modifiable, just by adopting the right feeding habits, having regular exercises and reduction of alcohol intake not forgetting tobacco smoking. Management of this condition is quite stressful and expensive but preventing it is a cheap as the air we breathe. We can combat the high prevalence of hypertension and avoid its complications too, thus having reducing premature death.

Who is at risk?

Anyone, everyone can get hypertension

Modifiable risk factors.

These are factors that can be avoided or stopped if already a victim.
Tobacco use, physical inactivity, unhealthy diet and excessive alcohols intake

Metabolic factors

These also fall under the modifiable factors
Raised blood pressure puts one at risk of getting hypertension
Overweight or obesity
Hyperglycemia (high blood glucose levels)
Hyperlipidemia (high levels of fat in blood)

Non modifiable risks factors.

These can also be unchangeable, or natural
Age
Genetics
Co-existing disease

Nutrition for Hypertention

Managing Hypertension

Nutritional consideration for managing hypertension and prevention

Nutritional factors play a great role in not only reducing the risk of getting hypertension but also managing the condition after diagnosis. DASH (dietary approaches to stop hypertension) focuses on having more fruits and vegetables, less sodium and saturated fat intake. These play a great role in the fight of hypertension

Restrict sodium intake

This can be achieved through putting less salt in the food, avoid added salt on plate and packed foods and beverages which contain great amount of sodium. If buying packed foods consider reading the labels to ensure there is less sodium content

Increase potassium intake

Potassium helps to reduce blood pressure and it's related complications such as stroke through improved vasodilation. This can be achieved through having fruits and vegetables such as bananas etc. For they contain a lot of potassium

Go for whole grains

Whole grains and cereals such as wheat, millet, oats, maize and avoid refined cereals such as refined maize flour, white bread

Plant based proteins

These could be a great option for they not only contain a lot of potassium but also free from cholesterol which increases the risk of blood pressure. On addition, they contain less sodium and reduces the viscosity of blood, making it easier to flow within vessels this low blood pressure

Vegetables and fruits

These contain great amounts of vitamins, mineral salts and nitrates. These include leafy vegetables such as cabbage, spinach etc. These provide a good vasodilation effect this reducing blood pressure

Limit alcohol consumption

Alcohol generally increases the cortisol levels which later increase the blood pressure. The calcium concentration level rises with alcohol consumption which constrict the blood vessels this elevated blood pressure. Besides that it leads to weight gain and it's a great risk factor for hypertension
