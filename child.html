Child Health

Growth & Development

Children, go through various changes and the greatest concern at this time is their growth and development and like it or not, nutrition contributes a lot to this.

Nutrients

The nutrients got from the various foods enrich the cells and tissues, and use them to develop the different organs and the physical growth that we see. Not forgetting the different stages and milestones that these children go through, ie sitting, crawling, walking, developing of teeth and so on.

Feeding & Nutrition

When a child is not feed well or not receiving the right quality and quantity of required nutrients, their growth and development will definitely be affected and the bad news is that these side effects will not only be seen at the young age but proceed to adult hood too. Such as neurocognitive development, malnourishment, resistance to diseases and other chronic diseases.

Therefore, to every parent, caretaker, guardians and teachers, as we plan or create menus for our children, we should put emphasis to what, when and how many times a child is eating depending on the different stages.

0 - 6 Months

Breast Feeding

UNICEF

For the first six crucial moments of life, medical experts and different organizations such as UNICEF agreed that the gold standard food of choice is mothers’ milk and thus encouraging exclusive breastfeeding.

Hydrating Baby

Do you know why? This is because it contains the exactly correct proportions of fat, cholesterol, protein and carbohydrates for the baby. And this also includes water, so no need of giving the baby extra water.

Breast Milk

Breast milk is not a perfect nutritional source but also contains antibodies that provides protection against several diseases. Mother’s milk is also cheap and requires neither ingredients nor time to prepare.

Breast Feeding Tips

Therefore, we need to ensure the child successfully breast feeds and how can we attain this?
Attachment and positioning aids successful breast feeding and also prevent mothers from getting sores which leads to painful breastfeeding.
There are various ways we can position ourselves and the child during breast feeding, but there are major signs of proper breast attachment.

1

The baby’s mouth should be widely open

2

Their chin should be touching your breast

3

The top and bottom lips should be curls outwards.

4

You see more of the darker skin (areolar) above the baby’s mouth than below

5

The cheeks of the baby should be full and rounded, you should not see the cheeks dimpling when your baby sucks.

6

The baby takes deep breaths during breast feeding and you can hear her or him swallow.

Once the baby has had enough, he/she will leave the breast by itself and most times fall asleep.
Well, one may ask how many times a baby should breast feed in a day? The answer is a baby should breast feed whenever she wants and at least 8-12 times in a day.

With the current situation where mothers have to work, making up to this frequency may be difficult to comply with and thus expressing may be an option, but endeavor the milk is kept in a cool place and should not stay for more than 6-8 hours. And bottles have to be kept clean to avoid chances of infection.

Try hard to avoid supplementing cows’ milk and goats’ milk on the baby’s diet, for this milk is meant for calves and kids not babies. Having less time to breast feed and reduced weight gain may force one to look for supplements such as milk formulas.

Ensure to get the right formular and follow the instructions of preparation. But please don’t replace breast milk with formulas, because it remains the standard and best food for babies below six months.

6 - 12 Months

Nutrition & Feeding

This is when the green light is given to mothers and caretakers to introduce other foods into the child’s diet. This is because there is increased nutritional developmental needs which are not all meet by breast milk. But breast feeding should continue till twelve months or more as long as the mother desires.

At this time, attention is paid to what we are giving the baby and how its prepared.

More focus on preparation

But basically, we start with soft food for easy swallowing and digestion, and gradually upgrade to the solid foods. Ensure to give a child a balanced diet, all nutrients must be involved that is carbohydrates, protein, vitamin and mineral salts and fats. Small quantities are good to start with as the child gets used to these new foods. One can prepare a meal by combining one each group

Suggestion

A thick porridge is made out of flour of either (maize, soya, cassava or millet) sugar, oil, blue band, milk, groundnuts can be added to the porridge Mashed food i.e., a mixture of matooke, potatoes, cassava, posho (maize, or millet) and fish, meat, beans, ground nuts, or simsim paste. Also, you can add vegetables like greens, pumpkin, carrots, avocado, tomato can also be added into the mixture.

Benefits of complementary feeding.
- Ensures healthy growth and development of children through the variety

Nutrition sources
Carbohydrates Porridge, cereals, potatoes
Proteins Dairy products, eggs, meat, fish and poultry, legumes
Vitamins & Minerals smashed fruits & vegetables

Preparing a Healthy Day

One of the best things we can do for our children besides investing in their future, through education and savings, adopting good healthy eating habits could be gold.

These will continue till adult hood since health habits are more likely to stay with you when you learn them as a child.

Amidst the daily busy schedules, we can still ensure our kids are not just having enough to eat but health food. This will reduce the chances of developing malnutrition (over nutrition) and it's related side effects such as chronic diseases ( diabetes type 2 and hypertension).

Start with Breakfast

This is the first meal of the day, and we should ensure it containing all nutrients that's is proteins, carbohydrates, vitamins and some fats. Forexample millet porridge with milk, egg sandwich ( whole or wheat bread), plus fruits. These will also keep the child full for some time and eliminate the urge to look for snacks. But ensure to give them a fruit salad or other health snacks before lunch

Prioritize Meals

Making meals on time, and ensuring everyone in the house come on the dining table to have meals contribute alot to child's morale of eating. It provides them comfort, gives time to chat and interact with parents and siblings. One is also able to monitor the child's eating habits and also set example, remember children easily copy what they see

Get children involved

This should start from deciding on what you as a family, buying groceries and also.preparing them. As you do this, you can also explain the importance of each item. On addition, some of these vegetables can be planted and thus, involve them in planting till harvesting

Make gradual efforts to healthier foods

Take one step at a time, especially if you are just embarking on this health journey. Start replacing the unhealthy items with the health ones. Forexample
whole milk_______low fat milk
Soda_______water or sparkling water
Ice cream_____home made smoothie
White bread_____brown bread

Limit sugar in child's diet. Don't ban

Banning the sugary foods and snacks can cause cravings instead make them a regular treat instead of part of diet. The normal daily consumption of sugar for children is not more than 12gm =3teaspoons. And a tin of soda contains 40gm of sugar= 10teaspoons of sugar

Eat more fruits and vegetables

This can be facilitated by making deserts centered around fruits and make them more appealing. Keep the fresh fruits on hand ie, place baskets filled with different fruits and place them in a place they can easily see and get them. Besides, it will act as a reminder, as they see them. Hide the vegetables in other foods especially in their favourite foods. You can grate the vegetables and place them sauces, breads and other pastry. Design the plate( be creative)

Think twice about fat

Fats are also important part of a child's diet. Helps one get and stay full, lifts ones mood but should ensure you are giving the right fats that is unsaturated fats( monou saturated and polyu saturated fats). Examples of these include olive oil, nuts, pumpkin, fish

Snacking

This may sound sweet and exciting on hearing, that 90% of people can't finish a day without having and at times replace snacks with main meals. Yet on the contrary, a snack is a meal eaten between main meals. That is breakfast, lunch and dinner.

Regardless, people have different reasons of snacking, this could be due to hunger, thirst, boredom, culture, social reasons, marketing and many more. No matter the reason, we all have to mindful of the snacks we are grabbing especially calorie content, sugar and salt for these can endanger your life and increase risks of chronic illnesses especially diabetes and hypertension
